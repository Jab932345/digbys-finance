Preamble

This Passenger Conveyance Agreement (hereinafter "The Contract", "This Terrible Document", or "The Reason You Should Have Just Got The Bus") is entered into between Digby's Cab Co., a sole trader operation of considerable dignity, and you, the passenger (hereinafter "The Scrounger", "The Backseat Driver", or "Mate").

By existing within two metres of this vehicle, touching the door handle, or breathing in the general direction of the driver, you are deemed to have accepted all terms herein, including those printed in invisible ink on the back of a receipt from 2019 that neither party can locate.

Clause 1 — Definitions

1.1 "The Driver" shall mean the extraordinarily patient individual operating the vehicle, who has heard your story about the time you nearly bought a campervan at least four times and still reacts with appropriate interest.

1.2 "The Fare" shall mean the amount displayed on the meter, which is final, binding, and not up for negotiation regardless of how long you pause dramatically and stare at it.

1.3 "The Journey" shall mean any trip including: nipping to Aldi for one thing and coming back with six bags, going to the pub, being dropped off "just here" which is never just here, and the time you needed Morrisons at 11pm specifically for Wotsits.

1.4 "Reasonable Detour" shall mean no detour whatsoever. There is no reasonable detour. We are not stopping at the cash machine. You knew where we were going.

Clause 2 — Payment obligations

2.1 The Passenger agrees to pay in full without: "I'll get you next time", "I thought it was free cos you were coming anyway", "have you got change for a fifty", or the haunting silence of someone pretending to look for their wallet.

2.2 In the event of non-payment, the Driver reserves the right to mention it at every social gathering for a minimum of three (3) years, bring it up at Christmas dinner, and post a passive-aggressive story that everyone knows is about you.

2.3 Banter does not constitute legal tender. "Cheers big man" is not a gratuity. Thumbs up through the window as you walk away is not payment in any jurisdiction known to English law.

2.4 The Passenger acknowledges that "I'll Venmo you later" has a historical completion rate of approximately 11%, and solemnly agrees that this time will be different.

2.5 Paying in coins that total less than the fare while pulling a face that implies this is somehow the Driver's problem shall constitute non-payment under clause 2.2.

Clause 3 — Conduct in the vehicle

3.1 The Passenger agrees not to touch the radio. This includes: changing station, skipping songs, connecting via Bluetooth without asking, saying "have you got aux?", saying "oh I love this one" and singing badly, or rating the song as if on a panel show. You are a passenger. Act accordingly.

3.2 Window operation is at the sole discretion of the Driver. Requests to open, close, "crack it a bit", or "actually close it I'm cold" shall be evaluated case-by-case and may be denied without explanation.

3.3 The Passenger acknowledges that the passenger seat is occupied by the Driver's coat, and shall not relocate said coat without explicit verbal consent. The coat was there first. The coat has tenure.

3.4 Eating is prohibited unless: (a) it is chips and you immediately offer some, (b) it is a Greggs product and you also offer some. Eating anything with a smell is prohibited under all circumstances with no exceptions whatsoever.

3.5 The Passenger agrees not to put feet on the dashboard, slam the door, or recline during a short journey. They agree, frankly, to just sit normally.

Clause 4 — Navigation

4.1 The Passenger agrees not to offer navigational guidance including: "you can cut through here", "I usually go the other way", "my dad always goes via the bypass". The sat-nav is on. The Driver knows. Your dad is not here.

4.2 "Just here" is not a valid destination. "Somewhere round the back" is not a valid destination. "You know the place, near the thing" is not a valid destination.

4.3 The Driver is not liable for traffic, roadworks, the school run, that roundabout near the retail park, or Acts of God. The Driver is also not responsible for the A14, which is simply cursed.

Clause 5 — Conversation

5.1 The Passenger acknowledges the Driver may or may not wish to chat. Acceptable gambits: "alright?", "busy tonight?", comfortable silence. Unacceptable: immediately beginning a 45-minute anecdote about someone the Driver has never met.

5.2 The Passenger agrees not to fall asleep before paying. If they do, the Driver may honk, announce arrival at volume, drive around the block again on the clock, or sit in contemplative silence feeling profoundly disrespected.

5.3 Phone calls shall be under three minutes. No speakerphone. The Passenger shall not provide a running commentary of everything outside the window to whoever they are speaking to.

Clause 6 — Liability

6.1 Digby's Cab Co. accepts no liability for: lost items, forgotten items, items the Passenger is convinced were left in this vehicle but were definitely left in the last one, or the general chaos of the Passenger's life.

6.2 The Driver shall not be responsible for the Passenger missing events because they were "ready in five minutes" when evidence shows this consistently takes between eighteen and thirty-one minutes.

6.3 If the Passenger is sick in the vehicle, they agree to an immediate, automatic, non-negotiable, and explicitly unlimited surcharge. No ceiling exists. It includes cleaning, emotional cost, the lingering smell two weeks later, and the fact that this story will be told at every gathering for the next decade. The Passenger acknowledges this is reasonable because it is.

Clause 7 — Force majeure

7.1 Digby's Cab Co. shall not be in breach in the event of: act of God, severe weather, a song the Driver needs to hear the end of, or a drive-through queue nobody could have predicted. The meter continues running in all force majeure events. Non-negotiable.

Clause 8 — Governing law

8.1 This Agreement is governed by the laws of England and Wales, common sense, basic human decency, and the unwritten rules of not being a pain about it.

8.2 Disputes shall be resolved first over a brew, then if needed by whoever is bigger, then by a panel of three mutual friends who will unanimously agree the Passenger was unreasonable.

8.3 This contract was written by someone with strong feelings and access to a phone. No legal professional was involved. It is nonetheless morally binding and spiritually enforceable.

8.4 By signing, the Passenger confirms they have spiritually accepted every clause, including the chips clause, the bypass clause, the coat clause, and the unlimited sick surcharge. They agree to pay promptly and not make it weird.
